6G — The Next Wireless Frontier

The sixth generation of mobile networks promises speeds exceeding 1 Tbps, sub-millisecond latency, and ubiquitous connectivity that will merge the physical and digital worlds. Some fascinating topics worth exploring:

  • Terahertz (THz) communications and their propagation challenges
  • Native AI in the network: networks that learn and self-adapt
  • RIS (Reconfigurable Intelligent Surfaces): surfaces that shape the radio channel
  • Integration of non-terrestrial satellite networks (NTN) with ground infrastructure
  • Privacy and security in an omnipresent connectivity landscape
  • Energy efficiency and sustainability in ultra-dense networks

Segment Routing v6 (SRv6) — Simplicity at Scale

SRv6 represents a paradigm shift in how IP networks forward traffic, leveraging the vast IPv6 address space to encode forwarding instructions directly inside the packet. Key research areas include:

  • Data plane programmability with SRv6 Network Slicing
  • SRv6 interoperability with MPLS in legacy networks
  • SRv6 as an enabler of Traffic Engineering in 5G/6G networks
  • SRv6 header compression (SRH compression) for WAN efficiency
  • SRv6 Micro-SID: overhead reduction in high-density scenarios
  • SRv6 applications in Multi-Domain and Multi-Operator environments

6G + SRv6 Convergence

The intersection of next-generation wireless networks and new IP routing paradigms raises fundamental questions: How can SRv6 handle the extreme mobility and millions of slices that 6G will demand? What role does the programmable data plane play in the automation of cognitive networks?
